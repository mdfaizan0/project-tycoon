# Project Tycoon - Game Rules

This document covers the game rules pertaining to the "Project Tycoon" game.

This is a business simulation game similar to "Monopoly", hence, the rules are very much similar to it.

## Game Objective

In order to see thyself a winner, one must accumulate the most amount of money by Buying/Selling, Mortgaging, Redeeming, Trading properties, collecting rent, and developing them with houses and hotels.

## Game Setup

1. **Board**: The board is a square with 44 spaces, including 24 properties (6 per side), 5 railways, 3 utilities, 3 "Fortune" spaces, 3 "City Vault" spaces, 1 "Income Tax" space, 1 "Wealth Tax" space, and 4 special corner squares.
2. **Players**: 2-6 players can play the game.
3. **Money**: Each player starts with **$1400** (initial capital).
4. **Tokens**: Each player chooses a unique token.
5. **Dice**: Two six-sided dice are used for movement.

## Detailed Gameplay Mechanics

### 1. The Start
As soon as the game starts, each player's **dice** roll automatically. The player with the highest roll goes first. In case of a tie, the tied players roll again until a first player is determined.

### 2. Movement and Doubles
When your turn comes, roll the dice. Your token hopping to the calculated destination will be shown with a smooth animation and the landing tile will be highlighted.
- **Doubles**: If you roll the same number on both dice (e.g., two 4s), you move as usual and then **roll again**. 
- **The Limit**: If you roll three doubles in a row, your turn ends immediately and you are sent straight to **Prison**.

### 3. Buying and Auctions
If you land on an **unowned** property, you can buy it for the listed price. 
- If you choose not to buy it, the property goes to **Auction**.
- Anyone (including you) can bid in the auction. Bidding starts at any price. 
- The highest bidder wins the property. If nobody bids, the property stays unowned.

### 4. Rent and Ownership
If you land on a property owned by another player, you must pay them **Rent**. 
- **Double Rent**: If a player owns all properties of a **Color Set** but has not built any houses on them yet, the rent for those properties is **doubled**. The same will be mentioned on the property card.
- Rent increases significantly as **Houses** or a **Hotel** are built on the property.

### 5. Building Houses and Hotels
Once you own all properties in a color set, you can start building **Houses** to increase rent.
- You must build **evenly**: you cannot have 3 houses on one property and none on another in the same set.
- Once you have 4 houses on every property in a set, you can upgrade to a **Hotel**.
- You can build only on your **active turn**, i.e. before/after rolling the dice.
- **Mortgage Dependency**: You cannot build houses/hotels on a property if any property in that color set is **Mortgaged**.
- **Selling Houses/Hotels**: You can sell buildings back to the bank at any time for **50% of the original price**. For example, if a player paid $100 for a house, they can sell it back to the bank for $50.
- **Selling Evenly**: Just like building, you must sell houses **evenly** across the color set.
- **Trading Buildings**: Houses and Hotels **cannot** be traded or sold between players. They must be sold back to the bank first before a property can be traded or mortgaged.

### 6. Mortgages
If you need cash, you can **Mortgage** your properties.
- You get **60%** of the property's value back from the bank.
- You **cannot collect rent** on mortgaged properties.
- To "Un-mortgage," you must pay the bank the mortgage value plus **15% interest** on mortgage value.
- **No Buildings Allowed**: You cannot mortgage a property if any tile in its color set has houses or a hotel. You must sell all buildings in that set back to the bank first.

### 7. Prison
You can end up in Prison by landing on "Trip to Prison," rolling three doubles, or drawing a card from **Fortune** or **City Vault**.
- To get out, you can:
    1. Pay a **fine** before your next roll.
    2. Roll **Doubles** on your turn (you move but don't roll again).
    3. Use a **Free Premium Lawyer Card**.
- If you don't roll doubles by your 3rd turn in Prison, you must pay the fine and move.
- You can collect rent while in prison.
- You can trade, build/sell houses and hotels, mortgage/unmortgage properties while in prison.
- You can get **Free Premium Lawyer Card** from **Fortune** or **City Vault** cards.

### 8. Trading
Players can **base a trade** at any time. You can swap properties, money, or "Free Premium Lawyer" cards with other players. Both players must agree to the deal.
> **Note**: If a mortgaged property is traded, the new owner must immediately pay a **10% interest fee (on the mortgage value)** to the bank. They can then either pay off the mortgage now or keep it mortgaged for later.

### 9. Bankruptcy and Winning
- **Raising Cash**: If you owe money and have $0 cash, you are not immediately bankrupt. You must first sell houses/hotels and mortgage properties until you have enough to pay the debt.
- **Bankruptcy**: If you still cannot pay after selling everything, you are declared **Bankrupt** and are out of the game.
- **Asset Transfer**: If you owe another player, the same value of assets (money, properties, cards) are transferred to them, rest of the assets are returned to the bank. If you owe the bank, all your properties are returned to the bank and auctioned off.
- **Winning**: The last player remaining with money **Wins** the game!

### 10. Turn Timers (Online Play)
To keep the game moving, each player has a **limited time** (e.g., 60 seconds) to complete their turn. If the timer runs out:
- If a player hasn't rolled, the dice roll automatically.
- If a player is in the middle of a decision (like buying/auctioning), the game will automatically **Pass** or **Auction** the item to prevent stalling.

## Tile Actions Summary

1. **Fortune / City Vault**: A random card is shown and instructions start executing immediately. If a card sends you past **BEGIN**, you still collect your salary.
2. **Income Tax / Wealth Tax**: Required amount is deducted from your balance ($300 / $150). If you don't have enough, you must sell/mortgage assets.
3. **BEGIN**: Collect salary every time you land on or pass this space.
4. **Urban Plaza**: A safe spot, nothing happens.
5. **Utilities**: Rent is determined by dice roll. For example, 5x the roll for 1 utility, 12x the roll if the owner has 2 utilities and 25x the roll if the owner has all 3 utilities.
6. **Railways**: Rent increases based on how many railways the owner has. For example, $30 for 1 railway, $60 for 2 railways, $120 for 3 railways, $240 for 4 railways and $500 if the owner has all 5 railways.

## Optional House Rules (Toggleable)
These rules are not part of the standard competitive game but can be turned on during game creation for a custom experience:

1. **Urban Plaza Jackpot**: All taxes and fees paid by players go into a "Jackpot" in the center of the board. Whoever lands on **Urban Plaza** collects the entire jackpot.
2. **Double BEGIN**: Landing exactly on the **BEGIN** space doubles the collection amount.
3. **No Auctions**: If a player chooses not to buy a property, it simply remains unowned and nothing else happens.
4. **Loan Sharks**: Players can take small loans from the bank, but must pay back with high interest within 5 turns or lose a property.
